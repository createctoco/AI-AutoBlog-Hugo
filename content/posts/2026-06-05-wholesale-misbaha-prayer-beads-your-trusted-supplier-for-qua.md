### Understanding the Market: Misbaha vs. Rosary Beads

While both misbaha and rosary beads serve as tools for prayer and meditation, their construction, material preferences, and cultural significance differ. Misbaha, traditionally used in Islamic prayer (dhikr), typically consists of 33 or 99 beads, often crafted from natural materials like wood, olive seeds, or semi-precious stones. In contrast, Catholic rosaries feature 59 beads arranged in five decades, with a crucifix and centerpiece. However, the modern wholesale market increasingly sees crossover demand—churches and religious stores often stock both items to serve multicultural congregations.
