### The Stone Itself — Not Just Another Blue Bead

Sodalite is a real gemstone. It’s mined, cut, and polished by hand. The color ranges from deep royal blue to a lighter navy, often with white calcite veins running through it. That natural variation is part of the appeal. No two beads look exactly alike.

We use genuine sodalite — not dyed howlite, not resin imitation. Some suppliers pass off cheap substitutes. We don’t. Every bead is hand-carved and polished by our craftsmen. You can feel the difference. The weight. The coolness. The slight irregularity that tells you it’s real stone.

Our standard size for sodalite rosaries is 8mm. That’s the sweet spot. Small enough to handle comfortably during the Rosary, big enough to show off the stone’s natural beauty. We also offer 10mm for customers who prefer a chunkier feel. 6mm exists but feels too dainty for most adults. 12mm? Too heavy for everyday use, honestly.

### Durability That Matters for Daily Use

Rosaries get handled. A lot. They get dropped in purses, shoved in pockets, wrapped around rearview mirrors. Cheap beads chip. String breaks. Crucifixes snap off.

Not ours.

Each sodalite bead is hand-polished to remove sharp edges. The stone itself is a 5.5-6 on the Mohs scale — hard enough for daily wear, soft enough to work with. We string them on durable cord, not cheap thread. The knots are hand-tied, not machine-knotted.

We’ve had customers tell us their sodalite rosaries have lasted five, six, seven years with regular use. That’s the kind of durability your customers appreciate.
