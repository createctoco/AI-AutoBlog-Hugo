### How We Build a Confirmation Birthstone Rosary

Let’s walk through the process, because I think that matters to wholesale buyers.

First, the wood. We use natural hardwoods — olive wood from Bethlehem, rosewood, sometimes sandalwood. No resin, no plastic composite. Each bead is cut, drilled, and hand-sanded. You can feel the difference when you hold it. The grain is real, the weight is right.

Then the birthstone. We set the stone bead as the centerpiece — usually the “Our Father” bead, or sometimes the crucifix base. Depends on the design. Some buyers want a single birthstone. Others want a full set for a family rosary. We can do both.

The stringing matters too. We can tie Peruvian knot or flat knot — whichever you prefer. Peruvian knot is tighter, less likely to loosen over time. Flat knot lays flatter against the beads, looks cleaner for display. We ask our wholesale clients which they want before we start production.
