### The Chain Itself — Don’t Cheap Out

I’ve seen other factories sell “thick chain” that’s hollow. You squeeze it and it dents. That’s not what we do. Our curb chains are die-struck, not cast. Each link is cut from a sheet of brass or stainless steel, then shaped. It takes longer, but the chain doesn’t kink. Doesn’t break at the clasp.

We use a lobster clasp with a 5mm jump ring. That’s overkill for most people, but it means the chain won’t come off accidentally. One buyer in Germany told me his customer lost two medals because the clasp failed. He switched to ours. No complaints since.

### One More Thing About Saint Christopher Himself

I’m not a theologian. But I know that Saint Christopher is the patron saint of travelers, and that his name means “Christ-bearer.” The story goes that he carried a child across a river, and the child got heavier and heavier — because it was Christ, carrying the weight of the world. That’s why people wear his medal when they travel. They want someone to carry the weight.

That’s also why the oversized medal works. It’s a visual reminder that the weight is being carried. You see it. You feel it. It’s not hidden.

We get emails from customers who buy these for deployment gifts, for college send-offs, for road trips. One guy ordered 300 for his trucking company. He gave one to every driver. He said it cut down on accidents. I don’t know if that’s true, but I’m not going to argue with a customer.
