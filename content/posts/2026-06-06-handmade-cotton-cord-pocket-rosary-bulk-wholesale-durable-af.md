## Why Hand Knotted Cotton Cord Pocket Rosaries Are Our Most Requested Bulk Item

You wouldn’t believe how many churches and parish gift shops ask us for these things. Pocket rosaries — the kind you can shove in a coat pocket, a purse, or even a glove compartment. And specifically, hand knotted cotton cord ones. Not the metal chain type. Not the beaded wire kind. We’re talking soft, flexible, durable cotton cord with knots between each bead.

We’ve been making them for years. And honestly? They’re not going anywhere.

Let’s get into why wholesale buyers keep coming back for these. And what you should know before placing your order.

## Why Cotton Cord? Why Knots?

Plastic rosaries break. Metal chains tangle. But cotton cord? It bends. It twists. It survives being sat on, stepped on, and tossed into a bag.

Some of our customers tell us they hand these out to parishioners who travel. Military chaplains order them by the case. Youth groups love them because they don’t make noise during prayer — no clinking beads.

The hand knotted part matters too. Each knot is tied by hand, not machine. That means every rosary has slight variation. No two are exactly identical. Some buyers love that. They say it gives the rosary character.

We use real solid wood beads — not plastic, not resin, not that cheap imitation wood that flakes after a month. Our wooden beads are carved and polished by hand. You can feel the difference.

## Sizes, Knots, and What Sells

Here’s something we’ve learned from years of shipping bulk orders.

The 8mm bead size? That’s our best seller by a long shot. 10mm comes close second. 6mm is available but feels small — some people like it for kids or for very compact pocket rosaries. 12mm? Too bulky for a pocket, but we do get orders for it on occasion.

For the knots between beads, we offer two options. Peruvian knot — tighter, cleaner. Or flat knot — softer, more flexible. Depends on what your customers prefer.

We had a parish in Texas order 3,000 pieces last year. They went with Peruvian knots. Said they hold up better in humid weather.

## A Few Practical Notes

Cotton cord rosaries are washable. Not that you’d throw them in a machine, but a gentle hand wash with mild soap works. The wood beads hold up fine.

We’ve noticed that darker wood finishes sell better for men. Lighter finishes for women. But that’s a generalization — we’ve had plenty of exceptions.

The crucifix and centerpiece are metal. We use a durable zinc alloy with silver or antique bronze finish. If you need a specific saint on the centerpiece, let us know.
