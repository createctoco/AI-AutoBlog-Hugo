## Why Wall Mount Resin Crucifixes Sell Better Than You Think

Let me be straight with you. We get a lot of questions about rosary beads — that's our bread and butter — but the wall mount crucifixes? They're a different animal. And honestly, they've been flying off our shelves lately.

Not the cheap plastic ones. I'm talking about resin crucifixes that look like carved wood or stone. The kind that makes people stop and say, "Where did you get that?"

We make them here in our factory. Same workshop where we hand-finish our wooden rosary beads. Same attention to detail.

### The Resin Advantage Nobody Talks About

Wood is beautiful. We love wood. Our [Rose Fragrance Wooden Bead 6mm Holy](https://www.alibaba.com/product-detail/Rose-Fragrance-Wooden-Bead-6mm-Holy_1601140558861.html) rosaries are some of our most popular items. But for wall mount crucifixes? Resin wins.

Here's why:

Resin takes detail better than wood. You can get sharper lines on the corpus — the body of Christ — and more texture on the cross itself. Wood grain fights against fine detail. Resin doesn't.

And weight? A resin crucifix has heft. It feels substantial when you hang it. Not flimsy. Not hollow. That matters when a church buys 50 pieces for a new sanctuary.

We've tested both. Resin holds up better in humid climates too. No warping. No cracking. Just a solid piece that looks good for years.

### What Size Works for Home Use?

Most of our wholesale buyers order the 12-inch to 18-inch range. That's the sweet spot for above a doorway, in a living room, or in a bedroom.

Smaller than 12 inches? Gets lost on the wall. Bigger than 24 inches? Starts looking like you're running a cathedral out of your living room. Not that there's anything wrong with that — we've supplied plenty of actual cathedrals.

For home decoration, 14 inches is our best seller. We're not sure why that exact size. It just works.

### Wall Mount vs. Table Stand — Pick Your Battle

Some folks want to put the crucifix on a table or shelf. That's fine. But wall mount is different. It becomes part of the room. You walk past it daily. It grounds the space.

We make both. But the wall mount crucifixes with the angled bottom? Those are the ones that catch the light. The resin catches shadows in a way that flat pieces don't.

One thing we learned: make sure the hanging hardware is simple. Nobody wants to drill three holes and level a bracket. Our crucifixes use a single nail or screw. Hang it, adjust it, done.
