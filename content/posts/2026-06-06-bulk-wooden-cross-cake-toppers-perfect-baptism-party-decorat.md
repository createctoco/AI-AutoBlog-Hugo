## Wait, Are Cake Toppers Even a Religious Item?

Some people argue that a cake topper is just decoration. I don’t agree. The cross is the central symbol of our faith. Putting it on a baptism cake isn’t frivolous—it’s a declaration. It says, “This celebration is about Christ.” We’ve had Protestant churches buy them too, for confirmation parties and even wedding receptions. The cross transcends denominations.

And honestly, the wooden texture is part of the appeal. Plastic crosses feel cheap. Metal ones can be too shiny, too formal. Wood is warm. It’s earthy. It reminds you of the cross Christ carried. That’s not my marketing spin—that’s what a pastor from Louisiana told me. He said, “I want the kids to touch it and remember it’s real.”
