**Don’t buy cheap cast medals with blurry details.**

We see them all the time. Medals where Our Lady’s face looks like a smudge. Where the grotto at Lourdes is just a blob. Where Saint Bernadette is barely recognizable. Those are made from low-quality molds, often in plastic or resin with a thin silver wash. They flake. They break. They disappoint the person receiving them.

We don’t do that.

Our oval Lourdes medals are struck from solid brass, then plated. The detail is sharp. You can see the roses at Our Lady’s feet. You can read the inscription “Que soy era immaculada councepciou” — the words Bernadette heard. The back shows Bernadette kneeling, clear as day. That matters when you’re giving this to someone for a First Communion or a baptism.

**Here’s something people don’t think about: the size of the medal matters for the rosary.**

If you’re attaching this medal to a rosary, you need to match it to the bead size. A big 20mm oval medal on a 6mm bead rosary? Looks ridiculous. A tiny 12mm medal on a 10mm bead set? Also off.
