We’ve been in this business long enough to see what happens when someone buys the absolute cheapest metal crosses from a random supplier. The finish flakes off after a month. The clasp breaks. The customer comes back complaining, and you’re stuck replacing stock for free.

That’s why we focus on solid metal cross charms — brass, zinc alloy, sometimes stainless steel — with electroplated finishes that actually hold up. You can get them in gold, silver, antique bronze, even black nickel. And yes, the price per piece drops hard when you order 500 or 1000 units.

## Customization matters more than you think

Here’s something most blog posts won’t tell you: the knot matters.

We can tie our rosaries with Peruvian knot or flat knot, depending on what you want. The Peruvian knot is traditional for Catholic rosaries — it’s the knotted style you see in most churches. Flat knot is more common in modern designs. Some buyers want one, some want the other. We do both.

Same goes for the cross charms. Do you want a simple Latin cross? A Celtic cross? A crucifix with the corpus? We have molds for all of them. And if you’re ordering 1200 pieces or more, we can engrave your parish name or church name on the packaging.

MOQ for OEM with your logo is 1200 pieces. For our in-stock items, you can start at just 12 pieces. Buy more, pay less. That’s the whole game.

## One more thing about rosary assembly

If you’re buying bulk cross charms to attach to rosaries, you need to think about the loop. Some crosses have a small jump ring attached. Some have a hole drilled through the top. Some have a flat back with a glued-on bail. We can do all three. But we recommend the jump ring style — it’s the easiest to attach to a cord or chain.
