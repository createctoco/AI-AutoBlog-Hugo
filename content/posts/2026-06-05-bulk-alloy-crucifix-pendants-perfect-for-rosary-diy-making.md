### Key Specifications for Bulk Alloy Crucifix Pendants

Before placing a large order, wholesale buyers must evaluate several technical specifications to ensure the pendants meet both liturgical standards and assembly requirements.

- **Metal Composition:** High-quality alloy crucifixes typically use a zinc alloy base (Zamak) or brass. Look for lead-free alloys to comply with international safety regulations, especially if the rosaries will be used by children or distributed in schools.
- **Plating Options:** Common finishes include bright silver, antique silver, bright gold, and antique gold. Antique finishes hide fingerprints better and give a more traditional, reverent appearance. Ensure the plating has a protective coating to resist tarnishing over time.
- **Loop Size and Gauge:** The attachment loop (jump ring hole) must be large enough to accommodate a standard rosary chain link or wire gauge (typically 0.8mm to 1.2mm). A loop diameter of 3mm to 4mm is standard for rosary assembly.
- **Corpus Detail:** A clear, three-dimensional corpus is essential. Avoid flat or poorly defined castings. The INRI inscription above the cross should be legible.
- **Weight:** A typical alloy crucifix for a standard rosary weighs between 3 and 8 grams. Heavier pendants provide a more premium feel but increase shipping costs.

### Quality Control and Packaging for Wholesale Orders

When buying alloy crucifix pendants in bulk, quality control (QC) is non-negotiable. A single defective pendant—such as one with a sharp edge, a missing corpus, or a closed loop—can ruin an entire rosary assembly run and damage your reputation.

We recommend the following QC checks before accepting a bulk shipment:

- **Visual Inspection:** Randomly sample 5-10% of the order. Check for uniform plating color, absence of pitting or bubbles, and completeness of the corpus.
- **Loop Functionality:** Test every sample to ensure the loop is fully open and can accept a chain link. Closed or partially blocked loops are a common defect in cheap castings.
- **Plating Adhesion:** Perform a simple tape test. Apply adhesive tape to the pendant and pull it off. If plating flakes or peels, reject the batch.
- **Packaging:** For wholesale quantities (typically 500 to 10,000 pieces), crucifixes should be packed in anti-tarnish tissue or zip-lock bags within sturdy cartons. Individual polybags prevent scratching during transit.

Avoid abstract or minimalist crosses that lack a corpus, as these are not considered crucifixes for rosary prayer.
