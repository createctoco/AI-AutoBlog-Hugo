## A Word on Quality Control

I’ll be honest with you — we’ve seen some terrible gold plated crosses in our time. Plating so thin you can see the brass after a week. Chains that break at the clasp. Crosses that look nothing like the sample.

We don’t do that.

Every batch goes through a three-step QC process:
1. **Visual inspection** under bright light for plating defects
2. **Chain strength test** — we tug each clasp
3. **Pendant alignment check** — the cross should hang straight

We also do random batch testing for plating thickness. Our standard is 0.5 microns minimum. If a batch falls below that, the whole batch gets re-plated. It costs us more, but it saves you returns and complaints.
