## Why Wood Beads Work for Bulk Orders

You might think wooden rosaries are a niche. But look at the numbers. Last year, we ran 15 production runs of 8mm olive wood beads alone. Each run was about 5,000 pieces. That's a lot of Hail Marys. The thing with wood is, it doesn't feel cheap. A parishioner picks up a plastic rosary, it feels like a toy. A wooden one? It has weight, grain, a little texture. People hold onto it longer.

We get a lot of church procurement managers asking about durability. Here's the honest truth — wooden beads can crack if they dry out too fast. So we use a food-grade mineral oil finish. It seals the wood without making it shiny. I've had customers in Arizona, where it's dry as a bone, and they tell me the beads hold up fine after two years. That's because we let the wood acclimate in our workshop for at least two weeks before cutting. No rushing.
