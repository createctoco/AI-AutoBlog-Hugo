So we started using a thicker silver plating — not the cheap 0.3 micron stuff you see everywhere.

Because your customers aren’t going to notice the plating thickness in the catalog photo.  
But they’ll notice when the cross turns black after a few weeks of handling.  

We test each batch.

That’s not standard in this industry.  

### The cross bead itself — what we learned from a tough client

A few years ago, a distributor from Texas asked us to make custom polished silver cross bead spacers for a rosary kit they were selling to Catholic schools.  
They wanted the cross to be exactly 18mm tall, not 17mm, not 19mm.  
I thought they were being picky.  

Turns out, they were right.  

When you’re making a DIY rosary kit for kids or confirmation classes, the spacer needs to fit between a 6mm bead and an 8mm bead without looking awkward.  
Too small and the cross gets lost. Too big and it throws off the whole decade.  

So we adjusted our molds.  
Now our standard polished silver cross bead spacer is 18mm tall, 12mm wide, with a 1.5mm hole — fits most common bead sizes used in rosaries.  

We also made the back flat, not domed.  
Why?  
Because a flat back sits better against the bead, and it doesn’t spin around when you’re praying.  

Small details. But they matter when you’re making thousands.
