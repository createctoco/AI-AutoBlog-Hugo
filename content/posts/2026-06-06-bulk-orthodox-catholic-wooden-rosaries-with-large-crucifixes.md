## Here’s a Factory Secret Nobody Talks About

Most rosary factories use machines to carve the crucifix. We do too – for the first pass. But every single crucifix gets hand-finished. Why? Because machines leave sharp edges. We’ve had complaints from customers in Russia about “rough edges cutting into fingers during prayer.” So now, every large crucifix gets sanded twice. Once by machine, once by hand.

We’ve got a guy – Vasily – who’s been doing this for 12 years. He can spot a bad finish from three feet away. He’s the reason our [rosary necklace with Jesus cross pendant](https://www.alibaba.com/product-detail/Catholic-Rosary-Necklace-Jesus-Cross-Pendant_1601127964579.html) has that smooth, almost polished feel. Even though it’s just wood.

## The “Olive Wood Problem” – And How We Solved It

Olive wood is beautiful. But it’s also tricky. It warps easily if not dried properly. We lost a whole batch back in 2019 – 500 rosaries that developed cracks after shipping to Greece. The client was furious. Rightfully so.

Now? We kiln-dry the wood for 72 hours minimum. Every batch gets tested with a moisture meter. If it’s above 8% moisture content, it goes back in the kiln. Our [rose fragrance wooden rosary](https://www.alibaba.com/product-detail/Rose-Fragrance-Wooden-Bead-6mm-Holy_1601140558861.html) is made from a different species – but same process. No shortcuts.

One thing I’ll say about Orthodox buyers: they’re patient. They’ll wait 45 days for production if they know the quality is there. But you can’t mess up the first order. Because they remember. And they’ll tell their friends.

## Wait, Are Orthodox and Catholic Rosaries Different?

Short answer: yes. But not in the way most people think.

Orthodox rosaries (chotki) usually have 100, 150, or 300 knots. We make the 100-knot version with wooden beads. But here’s the thing – we’ve had Orthodox buyers who specifically request our Catholic-style rosaries with the large crucifix. Why? Because they use them for personal prayer. The crucifix is universal. The beads are just… beads.

We also make [Catholic scapulars](https://www.alibaba.com/product-detail/Catholic-Scapulars-Worship-Embroidery-Liturgical-Stole_1601321075842.html) and liturgical items. But the bulk of our wholesale orders – about 60% – are wooden rosaries. And of those, maybe 30% go to Orthodox churches.
