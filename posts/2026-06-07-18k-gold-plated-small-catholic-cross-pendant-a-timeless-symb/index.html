What Makes Our 18k Gold Plated Cross Pendants Different
#

First off — the base metal matters. We use a solid brass core, then electroplate with genuine 18k gold. Not the “gold tone” or “gold wash” you see on low-end imports. Those fade. Ours hold up.

The size? These are small pendants — roughly 1.2 to 1.5 inches. Delicate enough for a child’s First Communion necklace. Substantial enough for an adult to wear daily. We’ve had buyers tell us their parishioners prefer this size over the big bulky crosses. It sits nicely on the chest, doesn’t snag on clothes.

And here’s something most factories won’t tell you: we hand-inspect every single piece. Yes, every one. The chain attachment point. The clasp. The plating consistency. If there’s a scratch or a thin spot, it gets pulled. Our reject rate is low — about 3% — but we’d rather scrap a bad piece than ship it.

We also offer customization. Want your church name or parish logo stamped on the pendant back? We can do that. Want a specific chain length? 16 inches, 18 inches, 20 inches — your call. We’ve shipped to parishes in Ireland, the Philippines, the US, and Canada. Each order slightly different. That’s fine by us.

A Word on Quality Control
#

I’ll be honest with you — we’ve seen some terrible gold plated crosses in our time. Plating so thin you can see the brass after a week. Chains that break at the clasp. Crosses that look nothing like the sample.

We don’t do that.

Every batch goes through a three-step QC process:

  1. Visual inspection under bright light for plating defects
  2. Chain strength test — we tug each clasp
  3. Pendant alignment check — the cross should hang straight

We also do random batch testing for plating thickness. Our standard is 0.5 microns minimum. If a batch falls below that, the whole batch gets re-plated. It costs us more, but it saves you returns and complaints.
