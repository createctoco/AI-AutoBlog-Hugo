Why bulk orders for Lent and Easter actually matter more than Christmas
#

Here’s a thing most people don’t realize. Christmas gifts fly off shelves, sure. But Lent and Easter? That’s when churches do their heavy lifting. You’ve got RCIA candidates receiving rosaries for the first time. You’ve got confirmation classes. You’ve got Easter Vigil where a parish might need 200 rosaries at once — not for sale, but as gifts for new Catholics.

We had a church in Texas last year order 1,500 pieces of our Catholic Rosary Prayer Beads 8-10mm for their Easter season outreach. They handed them out to every family that came to Mass on Easter Sunday. The pastor told me later that it brought in 40 new regular attendees. I’m not making that up. A rosary is not just a product — it’s a door opener.

And here’s the thing about bulk pricing for churches. They don’t have huge budgets. So we keep our wholesale minimums low — sometimes just 50 pieces for a small parish. But if you’re an importer ordering 10,000 units, we can drop the per-unit cost by 30-40%. That’s the difference between a gift shop making 50% margin or 200% margin.

I’m going to share some numbers from our actual sales last year, January through April. Because I hate it when suppliers just talk vague.

Second place? The Catholic Rosary Necklace Jesus Cross Pendant. That one surprised me, honestly. I thought the traditional rosary would win. But younger buyers — especially millennials and Gen Z — want something they can wear. So we added a stainless steel chain option. Sold out twice.

I’m gonna be honest here. Every single year, without fail, we get a panicked call around Palm Sunday. “We need 500 rosaries by Good Friday. Can you do it?”

Most factories will say no. We say yes — but there’s a catch. We keep a buffer stock of about 5,000 finished rosaries in our warehouse year-round. Not everything, but the core SKUs. So if you need 200 of the Catholic Scapulars Worship Embroidery Liturgical Stole — that’s usually ready to ship within 48 hours. The scapulars are actually one of our fastest-moving items for Lent. People wear them under their clothes as a devotion.

But here’s the thing. If you want custom packaging, or special crucifix styles, or engraved beads — you need to order at least 6 weeks out. Some of them have been with me for over a decade. They know how to tie a rosary knot so it never comes loose. That’s not something you can rush.

I remember one year, a customer from Ireland needed 1,000 rosaries for a St. Patrick’s Day event that was also Lent. He ordered on March 1st. We shipped on March 10th via DHL. Cost him a fortune in shipping, but he got them. He’s still a repeat buyer. Sometimes you just gotta bite the bullet on freight.

A few things church procurement people often forget to ask
#

Let me save you some headaches. I’ve dealt with hundreds of church buyers, and there’s three questions they almost never ask — but should.

First: what’s the lead time for your factory in China? Not just shipping time. Actual production time. We run at about 15-20 days for custom orders, 5-7 days for stock items. But if you order during Chinese New Year (which often falls right before Lent), add 2 weeks. Our factory shuts down for 10 days. Plan around that.

Second: are the crucifixes blessed? We don’t bless them at the factory. That’s a church thing. But we can include a note that says “ready for blessing” or we can work with a local priest if you’re ordering in huge volumes. We did that once for a diocese in the Philippines — 20,000 pieces. The bishop blessed them at a special Mass. We sent a video. It was beautiful.

Third: what about mixed SKUs in one order? You don’t have to buy 500 of one design. We do mixed pallets. You want 100 of the rose fragrance rosary, 200 of the olive wood, 150 of the crucifix necklace, and 50 scapulars? Fine. We’ll pack them together. Minimum for mixed is usually 200 total pieces. That’s how most of our church customers order.
