Those Tiny Enamel Cross Charms Keep Selling Out – Here’s Why
#

Look, I’ll be straight with you. We’ve been making rosaries and religious goods since 2008, and I still get surprised by what moves. Last month, a church group from Texas ordered 12,000 tiny enamel cross charms. Not rosaries. Not crucifixes. Just the little crosses. They told me these go into craft kits for Sunday school kids. I’ve got another customer in the Philippines who buys them by the kilo – she makes bracelets for a retreat center.

These aren’t your average bulk charms. They’re enamel-filled, about 15mm tall, with a clean loop on top. The enamel doesn’t chip easily, which matters when kids are involved. We’ve tested them by tossing a handful into a tumbler with rocks. Sounds stupid, I know. But we wanted to see. After 20 minutes, maybe two or three had tiny scratches. The rest? Fine.

Our factory in Putian runs about 8 production lines. Half of them are dedicated to rosaries, the other half to small metal goods like these crosses. The enamel process is actually pretty straightforward – we stamp the base shape, apply the color, then bake it at high temp. Each batch takes about 6 hours from start to finish. For a bulk order of 50,000 pieces, we’re looking at 3-4 days of production.

You want numbers? Here’s a real one. Last year, we shipped 340,000 of these enamel cross charms total. That’s across maybe 15 different colors. White is still the most popular. Then blue. Then red. But gold enamel? That’s catching up. I’ve seen a lot of church supply catalogs, and gold is showing up more in confirmation gifts and first communion jewelry.

Why Church Buyers and Importers Keep Coming Back
#

I had a conversation with a buyer from Ireland last week. She runs an online shop for Catholic gifts. Her problem? She was getting charms from a supplier in India, but the quality was all over the place. Some batches had bubbles in the enamel. Others had rough edges that would snag on clothing. She switched to us after a sample run. Now she orders every 6 weeks, about 5,000 pieces at a time.

We don’t just sell the charms loose. If you need them on a card, we can do that. If you want them with a small chain, we’ve got that too. One customer in Brazil wanted them attached to rosary chains as centerpieces – that’s how they use them for their community prayer groups. We made a custom die for that.

Here’s something else. The loop size matters more than people think. Too small, and you can’t thread a jump ring through. Too big, and it looks clunky on a bracelet. Ours are 4mm inner diameter. That’s the sweet spot for most jewelry craft work. We’ve had customers complain about other suppliers having 2.5mm loops – basically useless for anything except the thinnest chain.

What to Watch Out for When Ordering Bulk
#

One thing I’ll tell you – don’t assume all enamel crosses are the same. There’s a difference between cold enamel and hard enamel. Cold enamel is cheaper, but it scratches. Hard enamel is what we use. It’s baked, it’s smooth, it’s durable. You pay a bit more per piece, but you don’t get returns.

Also, packaging. We can pack them in poly bags, but if you want them on cards or in small boxes, tell us upfront. Our standard packing is 100 pieces per bag. For larger quantities, we vacuum-seal them to prevent tarnishing. Yes, even though they’re enamel, the metal base can oxidize over time if exposed to humidity.

Another thing – lead time. Right now, we’re running about 15 working days for a custom color order. Standard colors (white, blue, red, gold, black) are faster, maybe 10 days. I say “maybe” because sometimes shipments get held up at the port. That’s beyond our control. But once it leaves our factory, we track it.
